DeFi Yield Strategies with Olbra Stablecoins

Explore the various ways to earn yield on your PLNY, EURY, and USDY holdings through staking, liquidity provision, lending, and more within the Olbra DeFi ecosystem.

OF
Olbra Finance Team
November 15, 2024 10 min read

One of the key advantages of holding stablecoins in DeFi rather than fiat in a bank account is the ability to earn meaningful yield while maintaining price stability. This guide covers the various yield opportunities available within the Olbra ecosystem and helps you understand the risk-reward tradeoffs of each approach.

Understanding DeFi Yield

Before diving into specific strategies, it's important to understand where yield comes from in DeFi. Unlike traditional finance where interest is often subsidized or created through fractional reserve lending, DeFi yield typically comes from:

  • Trading Fees: When you provide liquidity to a trading pair, you earn a share of the fees paid by traders.
  • Borrowing Interest: Lending protocols let borrowers pay interest to access your deposited assets.
  • Protocol Incentives: New protocols often distribute tokens to attract liquidity and users.
  • Real Yield: Revenue from actual protocol operations distributed to stakeholders.

Strategy 1: Protocol Staking

The simplest way to earn yield is through Olbra's native staking mechanism. Deposit your stablecoins directly into Olbra's staking contracts to earn rewards:

  • How it works: Deposit PLNY, EURY, or USDY into the staking contract. Rewards accrue based on your share of the pool and are claimable at any time.
  • Expected yield: Variable, typically 3-8% APY depending on total staked amount and protocol revenue.
  • Risk level: Low. Smart contract risk only—no impermanent loss or counterparty exposure.
  • Best for: Users who want passive income with minimal complexity.

Strategy 2: Liquidity Provision

Providing liquidity to decentralized exchanges (DEXs) is one of the most common DeFi yield strategies. You deposit pairs of tokens into liquidity pools and earn trading fees.

Stablecoin-Stablecoin Pairs: Pairs like PLNY/EURY or PLNY/USDY are ideal for stablecoin holders because both assets maintain their peg, minimizing impermanent loss risk. These pools typically earn 5-15% APY from trading fees alone.

Stablecoin-Volatile Pairs: Pairing PLNY with ETH or other volatile assets offers higher potential returns but exposes you to impermanent loss. Only consider these if you understand the risks.

  • Expected yield: 5-20% APY depending on trading volume and pair volatility.
  • Risk level: Low for stable-stable pairs, medium-high for stable-volatile pairs.
  • Best for: Users comfortable with DEX mechanics who want higher yields than staking.

Strategy 3: Lending Protocols

Lending protocols allow you to deposit stablecoins and earn interest from borrowers. This is similar to how traditional banking works, but with transparent on-chain mechanics.

  • How it works: Deposit PLNY into a lending pool. Borrowers post collateral and pay interest to borrow your stablecoins. Interest rates adjust based on supply and demand.
  • Expected yield: 2-10% APY, highly variable based on market demand for borrowing.
  • Risk level: Low-medium. Smart contract risk plus potential bad debt if borrower liquidations fail.
  • Best for: Users who want straightforward yield without managing LP positions.

Strategy 4: Yield Aggregators

Yield aggregators automatically move your deposits between different protocols to optimize returns. They handle the complexity of finding the best yields and compounding rewards.

  • How it works: Deposit stablecoins into an aggregator vault. The protocol automatically deploys funds to the highest-yielding opportunities and compounds returns.
  • Expected yield: Variable, typically 5-15% APY after fees.
  • Risk level: Medium. Combines risks from multiple underlying protocols plus the aggregator's own smart contracts.
  • Best for: Users who want optimized yields without active management.

Strategy 5: Leveraged Strategies

For advanced users, leveraged strategies can amplify yields—but also amplify risks. These typically involve borrowing against deposited collateral to increase position sizes.

Example: Deposit PLNY as collateral, borrow USDY at a lower rate than PLNY staking yields, stake the borrowed USDY, and repeat. This creates a leveraged staking position that amplifies returns.

  • Expected yield: 10-30% APY or higher, depending on leverage.
  • Risk level: High. Liquidation risk, smart contract risk, and potential for losses exceeding initial deposit.
  • Best for: Experienced DeFi users only.

Risk Management

Regardless of which strategy you choose, risk management is essential:

  • Diversify: Don't put all your stablecoins in one protocol or strategy. Spread across multiple approaches.
  • Start Small: Test strategies with small amounts before committing significant capital.
  • Understand Smart Contract Risk: Even audited contracts can have vulnerabilities. Only use protocols with strong track records.
  • Monitor Positions: DeFi conditions change rapidly. Regularly check your positions and adjust as needed.
  • Be Skeptical of High Yields: If an APY seems too good to be true, it probably is. Unsustainable yields often indicate high risk or token emissions that will dilute value.

Getting Started

Ready to put your stablecoins to work? We recommend starting with Protocol Staking—it's the simplest approach with the lowest risk profile. As you gain experience and comfort with DeFi mechanics, you can explore more complex strategies.
