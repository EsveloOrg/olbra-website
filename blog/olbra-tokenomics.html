$OLBRA Tokenomics Explained

A comprehensive guide to the $OLBRA token—its utility, distribution, staking mechanisms, and role in governing the Olbra ecosystem.

OF
Olbra Finance Team
December 5, 2025 7 min read

While our stablecoins (PLNY, EURY, USDY) provide stability and utility for everyday transactions, the $OLBRA token serves a different purpose: it's the governance and value accrual mechanism for the entire Olbra ecosystem. This article breaks down everything you need to know about $OLBRA tokenomics.

Token Utility

$OLBRA isn't just a speculative asset—it has concrete utility within the Olbra ecosystem:

  • Governance: Token holders can vote on protocol proposals, including fee structures, new feature implementations, reserve management policies, and ecosystem fund allocations.
  • Fee Sharing: A portion of protocol fees generated from minting/redemption, trading, and other services is distributed to staked $OLBRA holders.
  • Staking Rewards: Stake $OLBRA to earn additional tokens from the ecosystem rewards pool, incentivizing long-term holding and participation.
  • Fee Discounts: Holding $OLBRA provides tiered discounts on platform fees, making it beneficial for active users.

Total Supply and Distribution

The total supply of $OLBRA is fixed at 1 billion tokens. There will never be additional minting beyond this cap, ensuring scarcity and predictable tokenomics. Here's how the supply is allocated:

  • Community & Ecosystem (40%): Reserved for ecosystem incentives, liquidity mining, grants, and community initiatives. Released gradually over 5 years.
  • Team & Advisors (20%): Allocated to founders, employees, and advisors. Subject to a 4-year vesting schedule with a 1-year cliff.
  • Private Sale (15%): Early investors who funded development. 2-year vesting with 6-month cliff.
  • Treasury (15%): Controlled by governance for strategic initiatives, partnerships, and market operations.
  • Public Sale (10%): Made available to the broader community at launch.

Vesting Schedules

Vesting is crucial for ensuring long-term alignment between token holders and the project's success. Here's how it works for different allocations:

Team tokens are locked for the first year (the "cliff"). After the cliff, tokens unlock linearly over 36 months. This means team members only receive their full allocation after 4 years of continued involvement.

Private sale tokens have a 6-month cliff followed by 18 months of linear vesting. This protects public market participants from immediate selling pressure while still rewarding early supporters.

Ecosystem tokens are released based on specific milestones and community-approved emissions schedules, ensuring they're deployed productively rather than flooding the market.

Staking Mechanics

Staking $OLBRA serves multiple purposes: it secures governance rights, enables fee sharing, and earns staking rewards. Here's how the system works:

  • Flexible Staking: Stake any amount with no minimum. Unstaking has a 7-day cooldown period to prevent governance attacks.
  • Reward Distribution: Rewards are calculated and distributed weekly based on your proportion of the total staked supply.
  • Compound Option: Auto-compound your rewards back into stake to maximize returns over time.
  • Voting Power: 1 staked $OLBRA = 1 vote. Voting power is proportional to your stake.

Fee Sharing Model

The Olbra protocol generates revenue from various sources, and a significant portion flows back to $OLBRA stakers:

  • Minting/Redemption Fees: Small fees charged when users mint or redeem stablecoins.
  • Trading Fees: Fees from DEX liquidity pools and trading pairs.
  • Lending Interest: Spread on lending protocol interest rates.
  • RWA Yields: Income generated from tokenized real-world assets.

Currently, 50% of protocol fees are distributed to stakers, 30% goes to the treasury (for development and operations), and 20% is used for buyback-and-burn mechanics to reduce supply over time.

Governance Process

$OLBRA holders govern the protocol through a structured proposal system:

  • Forum Discussion: Ideas are first discussed in the governance forum to gather community feedback.
  • Formal Proposal: Well-supported ideas become formal proposals requiring a minimum token threshold to submit.
  • Voting Period: Proposals are open for voting for 7 days. Quorum requirements ensure sufficient participation.
  • Execution: Passed proposals are implemented by the team or through smart contract automation.

Long-Term Vision

Our tokenomics are designed for sustainability, not short-term hype. The fixed supply, long vesting periods, and fee-sharing model create alignment between token holders and protocol success. As the Olbra ecosystem grows—more stablecoin volume, more DeFi activity, more RWA tokenization—the value captured by $OLBRA should grow proportionally.
